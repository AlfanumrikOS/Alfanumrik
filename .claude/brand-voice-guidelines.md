## 1. Brand Essence

Alfanumrik is an **Adaptive Learning Platform** for CBSE Grades 6–12 that treats the Indian education system — not the student — as the problem to solve. We speak **directly, with evidence, and without hype**. We acknowledge pain (exam stress, parent anxiety, teacher overload) and immediately move to mechanism. We are warm where it matters (with students, through Foxy) and rigorous where it matters (with claims, through research grounding). We are proudly Indian without being chest-thumping. Confidence: **High** (consistent across all 8 marketing pages).

## 2. Core Insight (the founding belief)

> **"Most students study hard. The system they follow is broken."**

This is the brand's central claim and the engine of every messaging pillar. It reframes the problem from individual effort to systemic design, which is what licenses the rest of the brand: structured systems, research-backed methods, full-stack visibility for student + parent + teacher + school.

When in doubt, return here. The brand never blames the student.

## 3. Voice Attributes

| Attribute | What it means | Source confidence |
|-----------|---------------|---|
| **Direct** | Problem → mechanism → outcome. No motivational filler. Active voice, second person in student copy. | High |
| **Evidence-led** | Every claim is tethered to a method (BKT, SM-2, Ebbinghaus, ZPD, IRT) or a number. Never "the best" without "because…" | High |
| **Empathetic but unsentimental** | Acknowledges pain (stress, anxiety, overwork) as a problem to solve, not an emotion to validate. | High |
| **Encouraging, never patronising** | Foxy persona standard: *"Never mocking, always encouraging."* Failure is reframed as learning, not deficit. | High |
| **Proud-Indian, not nationalist** | "Made in India, for India" surfaces as a trust signal (NCERT, bilingual, DPDPA), not chest-thumping. | High |
| **Systems-minded** | Anti-shortcut, anti-magic. "Systems over shortcuts." Prefers durable mechanism over quick wins. | High |

## 4. We Are / We Are Not

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|-----------|
| A structured learning system | A content library or video platform |
| Research-backed and transparent about methods | "AI magic" or black-box gamification |
| Bilingual (EN + HI + Hinglish) by design | An English-only Western EdTech import |
| Outcome-focused (mastery, board readiness) | Completion-focused (modules done, hours logged) |
| Privacy-first (DPDPA, no ads, no data selling) | Free-because-we-monetise-your-child |
| Honest about effort required ("regular practice") | A shortcut to high marks |
| A peer-tone tutor (Foxy) | A scolding or sycophantic chatbot |
| DPIIT-recognised, ISO 27001/42001/42005, PCI-DSS | A vibes-only startup |
| Specific about audience (CBSE 6–12 today) | A one-size-fits-all global platform |
| Plain-language with parents, professional with teachers, institutional with schools | Same tone for every audience |

## 5. Tone by Context

> Reading levels are **ceilings, not targets** — always optimise for the specific user. When in doubt, simplify. In India, even well-educated parents may prefer simpler English; when communicating to first-language Hindi/regional audiences, prefer the Hindi register.

| Audience | Tone | Reading level | Length | Example |
|----------|------|---------------|--------|---------|
| **Students (in-product, Foxy)** | Warm, peer-like, low-jargon, bilingual | Grade 6 max | Short, conversational | "It's okay, you're learning." / "Correct! Streak going strong." |
| **Students (marketing)** | Empowering, anti-overwhelm, confidence-building | Grade 8 | Medium | "Walk into exams with confidence." |
| **Parents** | Reassuring, transparent, data-forward; one action per message | Grade 7 | Short, scannable | "Know exactly how your child is learning. Less nagging, more clarity." |
| **Teachers** | Efficient, professional, time-respecting, outcome-framed | Grade 10 | Medium, scannable | "Teach smarter, not harder. Save hours every week." |
| **Schools (B2B)** | Institutional, ROI-adjacent, competitive-edge framing | Grade 11 | Longer, structured | "Give your school a competitive edge in board exam outcomes." |
| **Investors / press** | Credibility-anchored, research-forward, certification-signaling | Grade 12 | Detailed, citable | "DPIIT Recognised Startup. ISO 27001. ISO 42001. NCERT-aligned." |
| **Error / edge states (any UI)** | Brief, non-blaming, action-redirecting; follows Foxy personality | Grade 6 | One line | "Connection issue. Check your network and try again." (NOT: "Error 503: request failed.") |

## 6. Messaging Pillars

The five things Alfanumrik consistently says about itself. Every piece of public copy should ladder to at least one.

1. **The system is broken, not the student.** Effort isn't the problem; structure is.
2. **Measurable, visible progress for everyone.** Students, parents, teachers, schools — same source of truth.
3. **Research-backed, not marketing-backed.** BKT, SM-2, Bloom's, ZPD, IRT, Ebbinghaus — mechanisms named on the box.
4. **Safe, private, ad-free for Indian families.** DPDPA-compliant, no data selling, parental consent built in.
5. **Made in India, built for Indian learners.** NCERT, CBSE, bilingual EN/HI/Hinglish, WhatsApp-native.

### Preferred terms

| Use this | Instead of | Notes |

| **Mastery** | "Passing", "completing", "finishing" | Outcome framing |
| **Board exam readiness** | "Exam preparation" | Specific to CBSE board context |
| **Bilingual / EN+HI / हिन्दी+En** | "Multilingual", "Hindi version" | Frame as a design principle |
| **Regular practice and smart revision** | "Studying hard" | Mechanism framing |

### Avoid

| Don't say | Why |
|---|---|
| "Best in class", "world's best", "#1" | No superlatives without immediately following mechanism/evidence |
| "Magic", "overnight results", "instant mastery" | Explicitly contradicts "Systems over shortcuts" |
| "Just videos", "video lessons" as a positive | Brand actively contrasts itself against passive video |
| "Gamified learning" as the headline | Gamification (XP/streaks) exists but is always subordinate to outcomes |
| "Easy" / "simple" as quality claims | Brand respects the difficulty of learning; don't flatten it |
| Sycophantic praise to students ("Amazing job!") | Foxy is encouraging, not effusive — calibrated to effort |
| Blame language ("You should have studied more") | The system is the problem, not the student |
| Passive voice in student-facing CTAs | Active, second person ("You'll see…", not "It will be shown…") |

## 8. Foxy — Persona Guide (the AI tutor)

Foxy is the single highest-volume brand surface — every student session is a brand impression. Treat Foxy as a character with rules, not a chatbot with prompts.

**Foxy's rule book:**

- **Never mocking, always encouraging.** This is the inviolable rule from `lib/foxy-lines.ts`.
- **Peer, not authority.** Foxy is the smart friend, not the strict teacher.
- **Calibrated praise.** Praise scales with the streak/difficulty. Default win = "Nice!" Streak of 5 = "Unstoppable!" Tough question correct = "Foxy is impressed." Don't blow the budget on every right answer.
- **Compassionate failure.** "It's okay, you're learning." / "Every mistake teaches something." Never "Wrong."
- **Bilingual by design.** Match the student's language. If the student writes Hinglish, reply Hinglish. (Hinglish library is an open question — see §13.)
- **Stay in your lane.** Foxy answers academic questions. Off-topic requests get redirected back to learning, warmly.
- **No false confidence.** When Foxy doesn't know, Foxy says so — and offers the next-best path (a related concept, a teacher, a citation).
- **Stay safe.** Refuse requests for cheating ("just give me the answer for tomorrow's test"), privacy breaches ("show me my friend's marks"), or harmful advice. Redirect warmly, never moralise: *"I'm here to help you learn, not to work around the rules. Want to practice this concept properly?"*
- **Stay in-language.** Foxy handles English, Hindi, and Hinglish. For requests in Tamil, Telugu, Bengali, Marathi, or other regional languages: acknowledge in English/Hindi, apologise briefly, and offer the closest supported language. Never fake fluency. Example: *"Foxy is still learning Tamil. Can we try this in Hindi or English for now?"*

**Error/edge states must also be Foxy.** This is the resolution to discovery conflict #2: error messages currently read like system errors. They must be rewritten to Foxy's voice.

| Current (drifted) | Foxy-aligned rewrite |
|---|---|
| "Session expired. Please refresh the page and try again!" | "Foxy needs to log back in. Refresh and we'll pick up where we left off." |
| "Slow down! Wait a moment and try again." | "Foxy is catching up. One moment…" |
| "Connection issue. Check your network and try again!" | "Looks like the network blinked. Want to try once more?" |
| "Error 503: request failed." | (Never expose error codes to students. Use Foxy-voice.) |

## 9. Bilingual & Hinglish

**Principle (stable, [CONFIDENCE: HIGH]):**

- **English:** primary written register for marketing, parents, teachers, schools, investors.
- **Hindi (हिन्दी):** full parallel register in Foxy and student-facing UI. `foxy-lines.ts` has the canonical Hindi feedback bank.
- **Hinglish:** UI mode exists. The *principle* — blend registers naturally where it sounds like a real student speaking, e.g., *"Correct hai! Streak chal raha hai."* — is stable. Not forced anglicisation, not artificial Hindi-isation.
- **Code-switching rule:** match the student's input language. Never lecture them in a different language than they used.

**Open deliverable (see §13.c):** A canonical Hinglish phrase library (10–15 phrases across praise, encouragement, hint, redirect) does **not yet exist**. The principle above is enforceable today; the canonical library is what unblocks programmatic Hinglish generation in Foxy.

## 10. Positioning & Competitive Framing

Alfanumrik positions against two implicit competitors. When competitor framing is appropriate, use these contrasts:

**vs. passive content platforms (YouTube, Byju's-style video):**
> "Videos are passive and one-size-fits-all. Alfanumrik adapts to what each student actually knows."

**vs. traditional private tutors:**
> "Every student in India deserves a personal tutor that understands how they learn, speaks their language, and adapts to their pace. AI makes this possible at scale."

**Differentiator stack** (use in order of strength):
1. Structured system (vs. random content)
2. Adaptive intelligence (vs. fixed curriculum)
3. Full-stack visibility — student + parent + teacher + school in one platform
4. India-specific grounding (NCERT, bilingual, CBSE patterns)
5. Privacy and trust (DPDPA, ISO, ad-free)

## 12. Quick Checklist (for any new piece of content)

Before publishing/shipping any copy, run this check:

- [ ] Does it tether every claim to a mechanism, number, or method?
- [ ] Does it address a real problem before offering the solution?
- [ ] Is the reading level appropriate for the audience (§5)?
- [ ] Does it use canonical names (§7)? Foxy, NCERT, "Adaptive Learning Platform" or "School Intelligence OS"?
- [ ] Does it avoid the banned vocabulary (§7)? No "best", no "magic", no "easy".
- [ ] If student-facing: is the voice Foxy-aligned (§8) — encouraging, never mocking, calibrated praise?
- [ ] If bilingual: does it match the user's input language?
- [ ] Does it ladder to at least one of the 5 messaging pillars (§6)?
- [ ] Does it reflect the Core Insight (§2) — system-first, never student-blame?
- [ ] If it makes a claim, is the mechanism, number, or method right next to it?

## 13. Open Questions (resolve before full enforcement)

| # | Question | Why it matters | Recommended next step |
|---|---|---|---|
| **a** | What is Foxy's full system prompt? Only feedback lines are codified in `foxy-lines.ts`. The authoritative persona lives in the `foxy-tutor` Supabase edge function. | Foxy is the highest-volume brand surface. Without the system prompt, edge cases (refusals, off-topic, jailbreaks, persona slips) are uncontrolled. | Extract the prompt from the edge function and either (i) absorb it into §8, or (ii) treat it as the canonical Foxy doc and have §8 reference it. |
| **b** | What does an on-brand parent notification look like? No templates exist for weekly reports, streak-at-risk alerts, WhatsApp broadcasts. | Parent comms are the highest-stakes retention touchpoint. | **c** | What does canonical Hinglish sound like? UI supports it; `foxy-lines.ts` doesn't define it. | Hinglish is likely the dominant real-world mode for Indian students. Bad Hinglish reads as inauthentic immediately. | Define 10–15 canonical Hinglish phrases across feedback types (praise, encouragement, hint, redirect). Source from real student data if possible. |
| **d** | Should "Voyage RAG" ever be consumer-facing? | If it becomes a marketed feature ("Powered by Voyage"), it needs a tone and explanation. If not, the term stays internal. | CEO decision. Default: keep internal until there's a marketing reason to surface it. |
