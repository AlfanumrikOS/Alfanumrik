# Cold Outreach Email Template

Drop-in template for the founder to send after picking a firm from `02-firm-shortlist.md`. Square brackets are the slots to fill before sending. Total length ~200 words. Send from the founder's company email, not a personal one.

---

## Subject

`Engagement enquiry — Privacy Policy & Terms of Service review for Indian K-12 EdTech platform`

---

## Body

Dear [Firm name] team,

I am the founder of Alfanumrik Learning OS, an Indian K-12 EdTech platform serving CBSE students in grades 6-12, with a parent and teacher portal and paid subscriptions processed via Razorpay. We are preparing to publish updated Privacy Policy and Terms of Service documents and are looking to engage Indian-licensed counsel to review and finalise them before publication.

Engineering has produced detailed scaffolds covering DPDP Act 2023 compliance, IT Rules 2021, parental consent for minors, RBI e-mandate disclosures, refund policy under the Consumer Protection Act 2019, AI / cross-border transfer disclosures, and grievance redressal. The scaffolds contain marked items requiring legal opinion (29 substantive points across both documents) and company-specific facts I have already compiled.

We would value your firm's review on the marked legal questions, a redline pass on both documents, and a memo on any defensibility risks (limitation of liability, arbitration enforceability against minors, refund policy under consumer law).

Could you please let me know:

1. Whether your [data protection / TMT / payments] bench has capacity in the next two weeks;
2. Your typical engagement model for a scope of this kind (fixed-fee, project, or retainer); and
3. An indicative budget range so we can plan internally.

I am happy to share the scaffolds and the engagement pack on signing an engagement letter or NDA, whichever you prefer first.

Thank you,

[Founder name]
[Designation], [Registered company name]
[Direct email] · [Direct phone]
[Company website]

---

## Notes for sending

- Send Monday-Thursday morning, IST. Friday afternoon and Indian public holidays slow response significantly.
- Do not attach the scaffolds in the first email — wait for the firm to confirm interest, then share via a secure channel after engagement letter or NDA.
- If you do not hear back in 5 business days, send one polite follow-up. Do not send more than two follow-ups; move to the next firm.
- Keep the salutation generic ("[Firm name] team") rather than naming an individual, unless someone at the firm has been introduced to you.
