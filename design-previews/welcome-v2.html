What Alfanumrik does instead

i.

Ten minutes, then we stop.

Sessions are short by design. Our cognitive engine watches latency and accuracy and ends the session before fatigue sets in. The child leaves wanting more — that is the point.

CME · cognitive moderation
ii.

Mastery, measured by Bloom's.

Every question is tagged Remember → Understand → Apply → Analyse. We do not say "level up" — we say "she can now apply the photosynthesis equation; she still cannot analyse the Calvin cycle." Specific. Useful.

Bayesian Knowledge Tracing
iii.

One honest weekly note for the parent.

Every Sunday morning, in हिन्दी or English, you receive a short letter — what was learnt, what slipped, what to talk about at dinner. Written by the engine; reviewed by a human; never algorithmic gibberish.

The product is small on purpose. A tutor to talk to, a map to know yourself by, and a quiz that actually teaches you something when you are wrong.

i.

Foxy, who never sighs.

An AI tutor trained on the NCERT textbook for your grade. Answers in your language, never goes off-syllabus, never invents a fact. Asks better questions than it answers.
